## Product Positioning

MAGSNAP is a lightweight eyewear base with a standardized magnetic interface for snap-on modules.

The glasses are the base. The magnetic interface is the product.

Long-term positioning:

> MAGSNAP is a Wearable Magnetic Ecosystem.

Current sharp market expression:

> For lightweight POV/action-camera users and mobile creators, MAGSNAP makes mounting, using, removing, and switching small head-worn modules faster and more natural.

The base should stay simple:

- No battery in the base glasses.
- No processor in the base glasses.
- No display or prism in front of the eye.
- No sealed electronics in the base glasses.
- Removable modules carry the function.

The first understandable use case is product use:

- Snap on a module.
- Use it in the real world.
- Remove or switch it when the moment changes.

## Required Warnings And Disclaimers

The website must include safety and disclaimer content. Keep it direct and visible, not hidden as legal fine print only.

Required warning ideas:

- MAGSNAP is not protective eyewear.
- MAGSNAP is not medical equipment.
- MAGSNAP is not a promise of safe use in every activity.
- No prism, display, or lens sits in front of the eye.
- The camera/module should sit outside normal line of sight.
- Stop use if eye strain, double vision, headache, dizziness, or blocked vision occurs.
- Use only tested lightweight modules and mounting positions.
- Do not attach heavy, sharp, hot, or unverified objects.
- Keep magnets away from pacemakers, implanted medical devices, magnetic cards, and sensitive electronics.
- The base glasses contain no battery.
- Battery safety for removable modules must be tested separately.
- Do not use where distraction, impact, blocked vision, heat, water, or module failure could create risk.
- Users with a history of strabismus, amblyopia, binocular vision disorders, double vision, or other eye conditions should consult an eye-care professional before use.
- MAGSNAP is not recommended for children as a first target user group.

### Real World

Show real wearing, real switching, and real environments.

### What Can It Carry

Explain small module categories without overpromising:

- Camera.
- Light.
- Audio.
- Radio.
- Tracker.
- AI / future module.
- Maker experiments.
- Safety / work utility.

Use limits and testing language. Do not imply anything can be attached safely.

Module maturity rule:

- Camera and light are the easiest first modules for public understanding.
- Audio, radio, tracker, and AI modules can be shown as ecosystem directions.
- AI pod concepts may be discussed as validated concept direction, but current third-party AI pod products should not be described as mature.
- Battery modules require separate safety, charging, heat, shipping, and support evaluation.

### Frames

Explain the frame design choices:

- Large frame shape.
- Wide temples.
- Designed with wind, sand, and outdoor use in mind.
- Horizontal top line / helmet compatibility.
- Frame design is part of the system, not decoration.
